# Adapted Enterprise Architecture Artifact (AEA) – AI Governance Framework  
### Master’s Capstone Project – EU Business School  
### By: Kotha Sreeja

This repository contains the architecture models, documentation, and governance framework developed for my Master’s Capstone Project:

**“Adapting AI Governance Frameworks to the EU AI Act for Civil and Critical Security Systems: An Impact Analysis Study.”**

The project focuses on designing a **standards-aligned AI governance architecture** for a high‑risk maritime AI system, integrating the EU AI Act, ISO/IEC standards, and enterprise architecture modeling using NAF4 and Archi.

---

##  Project Overview

High‑risk AI systems require strong governance, traceability, and accountability.  
This project addresses the **preparedness gap** faced by operators deploying AI systems before full regulatory clarity.

The Adapted Enterprise Architecture Artifact (AEA) provides:

- A structured governance model  
- Clear accountability and role mapping  
- Standards-aligned processes  
- Traceable compliance chains  
- An interactive architecture model (HTML export)  

The AEA is designed as a **reusable consulting artifact** for organizations preparing for the EU AI Act.

---

##  What the AEA Is

The **Adapted Enterprise Architecture Artifact (AEA)** is a governance-by-design framework that integrates:

- **Organizational architecture** (roles, responsibilities, oversight)  
- **Process architecture** (AI lifecycle processes)  
- **Information architecture** (documentation flows, evidence artifacts)  
- **Standards & legal mapping** (EU AI Act + ISO/IEC)  

It ensures that every AI-related action is:

- Accountable  
- Traceable  
- Auditable  
- Legally aligned
